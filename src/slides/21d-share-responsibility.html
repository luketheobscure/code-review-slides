Share responsibility.

  • The author is responsible for what they submit. The reviewer is responsible for what they approve.
  • Code review is a conversation, not a gate. Both parties share responsibility for the quality of the code.
Code review has always been a shared act. The author drives clarity in; the reviewer drives clarity out. AI doesn't change that contract — it makes it more important, because the temptation to rubber-stamp is higher. If the author didn't understand the code, they shouldn't have submitted it. If the reviewer didn't read it, they share the blame when it breaks. AI review tools are useful but they don't hold anyone accountable. A human must. One team learned this the hard way — AI-owned PRs with no human face: "if one did get merged and caused problems, there was no one you could hold responsible."
