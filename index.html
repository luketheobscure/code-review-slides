Theory of Constraints

The bottleneck isn't
where you think it is.

🏭 [ Assembly line diagram — machines humming, one station backed up ]
A framework borrowed from manufacturing: the Theory of Constraints. Core insight: a chain is only as strong as its weakest link. Optimizing every other part doesn't help if the bottleneck stays the same.
"Maximizing every machine's efficiency actually hurts the system by creating pile-ups at the bottleneck."
More output upstream just means more waiting downstream. Faster code generation means nothing if review can't keep up.
"An hour lost at a bottleneck is an hour lost for the entire system. An hour saved at a non-bottleneck is a mirage."
This is the uncomfortable truth about giving every engineer a coding agent. You've made the fast parts faster. You haven't touched the slow part.

Simply giving every engineer a coding agent does not substantially increase output. You've sped up code generation — but review, comprehension, and verification haven't gotten faster. 38% of devs say reviewing AI code requires MORE effort than human-written code. Werner Vogels coined "verification debt" — comprehension comes with the act of creation. When the machine writes it, you rebuild that comprehension during review. The bottleneck has moved. It's now at review and verification — and we're flooding it.

Resist the pull toward large AI-generated PRs. Small changes are easier to reason about, easier to revert, easier to catch problems in. If you can't explain what a change does, it shouldn't go in. Be the shepherd, not the rubber stamp.

The Future — Smarter Infrastructure

AI code review —
with guardrails.

  • AI can catch issues at scale that humans miss
  • Don't let agents respond to each other
  • Hallucination laundering: one model's wrong answer becomes another's trusted input
  • A human must stay in the loop at meaningful decision points
AI can help catch issues at scale that humans miss — inconsistencies, common vulnerability patterns. Critical rule: don't let agents respond to each other. Hallucination laundering — one model's confident wrong answer becomes another model's trusted input. A human needs to stay in the loop at meaningful decision points.

The Future — Smarter Infrastructure

Verification, not review.

✅ [ Green CI pipeline — tests passing, contracts verified ]
  • The bottleneck is comprehension — don't solve it with more reading
  • Build tooling that verifies behavior rather than requiring re-derivation
  • Tests, contracts, property checks — these are your allies
  • Invest in verification infrastructure now, or pay interest forever
The bottleneck is comprehension. Don't try to solve a comprehension problem with more reading. Build tooling that verifies behavior rather than requiring a human to re-derive it from scratch. Tests, contracts, property checks — these are your allies. Vogels' framing: invest in verification infrastructure now, or pay interest forever.

The Future — Smarter Infrastructure

Review the whole system.

🗺️ [ System architecture map — zoomed out ]
  • AI changes are local — they optimize the piece in front of them
  • Someone needs to hold the map
  • Architecture & system-level thinking become more important as AI generates more surface area

AI changes tend to be local. They optimize the piece in front of them without seeing the broader system. Someone needs to hold the map. Architecture review, dependency review, and system-level thinking become MORE important as AI generates more of the surface area. The model doesn't know your system. You do. Don't outsource that.
